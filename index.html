The God of Recovery

God doesn't throw broken things away. He recovers them. He restores them. He rebuilds.

Opening Hook

What's the worst thing you've ever lost?

Think about the panic of losing something valuable. The frantic searching. The relief when you finally find it.

Today we're talking about God as a God of recovery. He doesn't lose phones or keys — but He does lose people. And what He recovers is way more valuable.

Point I
I. God is a God of recovery -Gen. 1:1-3; Ezra 9:9

In Genesis 1, the earth became waste and emptiness. Darkness was over the deep.

God didn't throw the planet in the cosmic trash. He recovered it. He spoke light into the darkness.

Ezra 9:9 - "God has not forsaken us... but has extended lovingkindness to us... to give us a reviving to raise up the house of our God."

If God's nature is to recover, what does that tell you about what He wants to do with YOUR life when you mess up?

Point II.A & II.B
II. In Abraham, God chose a new race...
A. God chose and called Abraham out of the land of idols -Gen. 12:1-3
B. They continually turned away from God, resulting in captivity -Neh. 9:24-30; 2 Chron. 36:14-16

Abraham: Called out of Ur (a city of idols) to a new land to be God's people.

Egypt to the Good Land: Israel was enslaved, but God rescued them through Moses and brought them to the promised land.

The Fall: Once in the land, they followed after idols. The kingdom split in two (North and South).

The Lowest Condition: They became no different than the Canaanites God told them to replace.

Captivity: Babylon came. The temple was destroyed. Jerusalem became a ghost town. The chosen race was carried off in chains.

Point II.C
II.C God recovered His people back to the land -Ezra 1:1-4; 2 Chron. 36:22-23

The Return: Ezra 1-3

70 years of captivity. Then God stirs up a pagan king, Cyrus of Persia, to issue a decree: "Go back. Rebuild the house of God."

When they arrived in Jerusalem, the city was in ruins. Weeds everywhere.

The first thing they rebuilt wasn't a wall or houses. It was the altar. Worship came first.

Then they laid the temple foundation. Some wept. Some shouted for joy. Recovery had begun.

Point III
III. The reality of God's recovery is the recovery of the church
A. God established the church -Eph. 1:23; 2:19-22
B. The church fell into degradation and division -1 Cor. 1:10-13
C. The Lord came in to recover the church -Ezra 3:1, 10-12

The Reality: The Church

The Old Testament is a picture. The reality is the church.

Established: God established the church as His true habitation.

Degraded: The church fell. Christ was replaced by good works, dead knowledge, the clergy-laity system, and division. This divided system is Babylon the Great.

Pursuing anything other than Christ is idolatry. And idolatry scatters.

Point IV
IV. We are in the Lord's recovery today

We don't have to look at history to see degradation—just look at modern Christianity. The churches are degraded today. Believers have drifted into religion.

35%
of self-identified Christians read the Bible at least once a week outside of church. Source: Pew Research Center, "Religion in Everyday Life" / "The Bible in America"
26%
of Americans say they read the Bible 3 or more times per year. Source: American Bible Society, State of the Bible 2023

The Word of God is collecting dust. If people aren't reading God's Word, what are they doing? They are practicing religion.

Religion is doing things FOR God, but WITHOUT God.

It's showing up to a building, singing songs, and listening to a professional speak—then living the exact same way as the world for the other 6 days. God doesn't want religion. He wants reality.

The Gospel & Application

He doesn't throw broken things away.

Man is fallen and separated from God — like Israel in captivity. But God prepared a complete salvation:

Judicially: He redeemed us with the blood of Christ. The legal price was paid.

Organically: He saves us in our disposition through the Holy Spirit. He changes us from the inside out.

Blood redeems. Spirit transforms. That's the complete recovery.

This week: Identify one "idol" — one thing that's been replacing Christ in your life. Ask the Lord to recover that area. He's the God of recovery. He doesn't give up on what's broken.
